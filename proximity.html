What is Proximity?

Textbook Definition

"We perceive objects that are close together as belonging to the same group. A related law of Common Fate, says that we perceive objects moving in the same direction as part of the same group"(pg 60).

"Placing elements in proximity to one another goes back to clumping. The idea is to avoid a busy cluttered layout by physically grouping items together that belong together"(pg 60).

White Space is Not Your Enemy by Rebecca Hagen and Kim Golombisky. Published in 2017 by Taylor and Francis Group, LLC.

Breaking It Down

Grouping text or visuals together will lead viewers to the assumption that they go together. Be cautious of spacing while working on your layout. Accidentally placing two separate thoughts close together will confuse your viewer.

Real World Example

The plates and bowls in the cabinet exemplify proximity. They represent two separate groups.

The spacing in between them makes it obvious that they are different. All the plates go together. All the bowls go together. There isn't a random bowl in the plate stack because that would lead to more confusion.
