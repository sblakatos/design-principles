What is Similarity?

Textbook Definition

"Our minds group things with similar properties, such as color or shape"(pg 62).

White Space is Not Your Enemy by Rebecca Hagen and Kim Golombisky. Published in 2017 by Taylor and Francis Group, LLC.

Breaking It Down

People recognize similarities within a layout and will group those similar items together. Whether it be color, shapes, or visuals, the assumption that they are grouped together will be made.

Real World Example

This image is a good example of similarity. Each different type of silverware has its own grouping. For example, knives are with knives and spoons are with spoons. They are even broken up by size as well.

Similarity is an association made in the brain. It's another things that people don't think twice about. When putting away silverware, you wouldn't just mix them all together. Separating them by groups allows for a nice flow.
