What is Movement?

Textbook Definition

"The principle of movement goes back to the idea that good design controls the eye's flow through the composition"(pg 57).

"Horizontal lines communicate movement flowing left to right or right to left"(pg 58).

"Vertical lines tend to communicate stability, such as tress and all buildings. Vertical lines also may communicate inspirational upward movement... or downward movement"(pg 58).

"Diagonal lines communicate exciting dynamic movement. Two converging diagonal lines communicate distance"(pg 58).

"The bottom line for movement and flow is that you want to move the eye across the layout in order to convey information as well as to evoke emotion"(pg 58).

White Space is Not Your Enemy by Rebecca Hagen and Kim Golombisky. Published in 2017 by Taylor and Francis Group, LLC.

Breaking It Down

Movement is crucial to a successful layout. Your reader should not have to stop reading to figure out where they should go next.

Feel free to use a mixture of lines however, your layout should never appear too busy.

The circular pattern is your best option because it will lead viewers to the beginning of layout once again, to examine it as a whole.

Real World Example

This tapestry is a good example of movement because it is easy to read. It is very simplistic which makes it easy for someone to follow.
